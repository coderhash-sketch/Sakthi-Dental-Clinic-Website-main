Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about your dental health.

Tooth pain can be caused by various factors including cavities, gum disease, tooth fractures, or an abscess. It's important to visit a dentist immediately to diagnose the root cause and prevent further damage.

Scaling is a professional cleaning procedure to remove plaque and tartar (calculus) from the teeth and below the gum line. It is necessary to prevent gum diseases like gingivitis and periodontitis.

Fluorosis is a condition that causes changes in the appearance of tooth enamel. It often occurs when children consume too much fluoride while their permanent teeth are still forming under the gums.

Modern dental fillings are virtually painless. We use local anesthesia to numb the area, ensuring you feel no discomfort during the procedure.

RCT is a procedure to save a severely damaged or infected tooth. It involves removing the infected pulp, cleaning the canal, and sealing it to prevent further infection.

We recommend a child's first dental visit by their first birthday or when their first tooth appears. Early visits help children get comfortable with the dentist and ensure healthy development.

Brush twice a day with fluoride toothpaste, floss daily, use mouthwash, and limit sugary snacks. Regular dental check-ups are also essential.

While orthodontic treatment can be done at any age, the ideal time is usually between 10 and 14 years when the head and mouth are still growing. However, adults can also benefit from braces or aligners.

Not always. If they are healthy, fully erupted, and properly aligned, they can stay. However, they often become impacted or cause crowding, in which case removal is recommended.

With proper care and maintenance, dental implants can last a lifetime. They are a highly durable and long-term solution for missing teeth.

You should replace your toothbrush every 3 to 4 months, or sooner if the bristles are frayed. It's also a good idea to change it after you've had a cold or flu.

Tooth sensitivity can be caused by worn enamel, exposed tooth roots, cavities, or gum disease. Using a desensitizing toothpaste can help, but a dental visit is needed to find the cause.

We generally recommend a check-up and cleaning every 6 months. However, some patients may need more frequent visits depending on their oral health needs.

In case of severe pain, a knocked-out tooth, or a broken jaw, call our emergency line immediately. Quick action can often save a tooth or prevent serious complications.
